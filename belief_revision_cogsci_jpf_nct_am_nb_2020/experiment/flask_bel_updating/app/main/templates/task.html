Imagine you are a political consultant travelling around the country for your political party.

You are helping the local branches of your political party decide between two candidates most suitable for public office.



You will travel to three different cities and the candidates competing for public office are different in each city.



Note that the candidates are fictional, so your real-world political opinions need not be involved in your judgments. Try to base them on the fictional evidence you will receive throughout the task!



Instructions: Overview

***Please remember to read all instructions carefully. You will have to complete a comprehension quiz at the end of the instructions.***

In each city, the local branch of your political party has already run two initial selection tests, with candidate A winning one and candidate B winning the other.

Your job is to use additional selection tests and the views of other likely voters from your party to break the tie between the candidates and make a final recommendation.

First, you will review some evidence about the two candidates’ performances on two selection tests assessing different qualifications needed for public office. Each selection test assesses specific qualifications of the candidates that are not covered in the other tests.

After reviewing the candidates' performances on the two selection tests, you will provide an initial recommendation.

Then, you will interview three likely voters from your party about their views of the candidates, as well as finding out about these voters’ relationships with each other and knowledge of the candidates.

Finally, you will update your judgment and make your final recommendation.

Before you start your own journey, let’s shadow a senior consultant on her trip to Denver and see how the process works!

Instructions: Summary

Well done! Denver appreciated the advice. Below is a short summary of your training, and there will be a comprehension quiz on the next page, after which you can get to work. Good luck!

1. You are a political consultant and you are helping local branches of your political party decide between two competing candidates.

2. You will review the candidates' performances on two selection tests assessing different qualifications needed for public office. Each selection test assesses specific qualifications of the candidates that are not covered in other tests.

3. After reviewing the candidates' performances on the two selection tests, you will provide an initial recommendation.

4. After providing your initial recommendation, you will interview three locals and learn about their beliefs and how certain they are about their beliefs. The information provided by locals is very valuable to you as their knowledge about the candidates' abilities is based on observing the candidates during debates in their local townhall. You won't have the chance to visit these debates by yourself, so make sure that you consider their beliefs and certainty ratings carefully.

5. Important: The three locals might have talked to each other prior to the interview. Moreover, they might have attended the same debate in their local townhall, meaning that their beliefs about the candidates' abilities are based on the same observations (specific details about locals' beliefs and their relationship with each other will be clear once you arrived in a city).

6. You will be able to revise your belief and certainty ratings after interviewing locals. This will help you to improve the quality of your advice.
